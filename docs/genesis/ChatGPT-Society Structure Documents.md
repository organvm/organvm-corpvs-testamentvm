Interpretation of the Layers
============================

**Physical / ecological layer**  
This is the material substrate of society. It includes territory, climate, ecosystems, and resource distributions. The documentary record consists of surveys, environmental reports, and geographic mapping.

**Infrastructure layer**  
Human-built systems that allow society to function: roads, electrical grids, communication networks, ports, logistics chains. Their documents are engineering plans, maintenance logs, and infrastructure registries.

**Political / legal layer**  
Authority structures governing the territory. The key documentary corpus includes constitutions, statutes, treaties, judicial decisions, and regulatory filings.

**Economic layer**  
Systems for producing and distributing value. Corporate charters, financial ledgers, contracts, property titles, and taxation records form the main documentary artifacts.

**Social / civic layer**  
Structures of everyday life: family, community, religion, health care, civic organizations. The documentation consists of vital records, medical histories, membership registries, and demographic data.

**Knowledge / culture layer**  
Mechanisms through which society stores and transmits knowledge. The documents are textbooks, research papers, libraries, archives, and media.

**Symbolic / mythic layer**  
The interpretive layer that gives meaning to all other layers. This includes myth, ideology, philosophy, national narrative, ritual, art, and symbolic systems.

* * *

Structural Principle
====================

The diagram expresses a **dependency hierarchy**.

Lower layers constrain upper layers.

```
environment → infrastructure → governance → economy → society → knowledge → myth
```

At the same time, higher layers reinterpret and reorganize the lower ones through law, planning, culture, and ideology.

This bidirectional interaction produces the **complete documentary record of a civilization**.
