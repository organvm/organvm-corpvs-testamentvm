1\. Nested Hierarchical Structure (Holarchy)
--------------------------------------------

Every environment exists **inside a larger environment while containing smaller systems**.

| Level | System Example | Relationship |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Subatomic | particles, fields | foundation of matter |
| Atomic / Molecular | chemistry, biomolecules | building blocks of cells |
| Cellular | organelles, metabolic cycles | units of life |
| Organism | nervous, immune systems | integrated living system |
| Ecosystem | species interactions | organisms interacting with environment |
| Planetary | climate, biosphere | regulation of global life conditions |
| Cosmic | solar systems, galaxies | astrophysical environment of planets |

Each level both **depends on lower levels and constrains higher ones**.  
For example:

Molecular chemistry → enables cellular metabolism  
Cellular metabolism → sustains organisms  
Organisms → form ecosystems  
Ecosystems → regulate planetary climate

This pattern is often described as a **holarchy**: parts that are simultaneously wholes within larger structures.

Systems connect primarily through **flows of energy and matter**.

Example chain:

Solar radiation

→ heat and waste returned to the environment.

At larger scales:

Solar input  
→ atmospheric circulation  
→ ocean currents  
→ climate patterns  
→ ecosystem distribution.

Thus the entire system is a **continuous transformation of energy across scales**.

3\. Information and Feedback

Examples:

Predator population increases

→ predator population later declines.

Vegetation growth  
→ alters atmospheric carbon levels  
→ changes climate conditions  
→ affects vegetation again.

Within organisms:

Environment stimulus  
→ neural processing  
→ behavioral response  
→ environmental change.

Feedback produces **dynamic regulation**, allowing systems to stabilize or shift.

These interfaces function as **transfer zones** where energy, matter, and information move between domains.

Emergence connects levels because **higher-order behavior arises from interactions among lower components**.
