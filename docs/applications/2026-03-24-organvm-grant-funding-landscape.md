## 1. NSF Grants

### 1a. POSE - Pathways to Enable Open-Source Ecosystems

**The single most relevant federal program for ORGANVM.**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Program** | NSF 24-606 |
| **Phase I** | Up to $300,000 / 1 year (scoping & planning) |
| **Phase II** | Up to $1,500,000 / 2 years (establishment & expansion) |
| **Phase I Deadlines** | September 2, 2025; January 13, 2026 (annually, second Tuesday in January / first Tuesday in September) |
| **Phase II Deadline** | September 2, 2025 (annually) |
| **Anticipated Awards** | ~20 Phase I, ~10 Phase II per cycle |
| **501(c)(3) Required?** | No. Nonprofits, for-profits, and IHEs all eligible |

**Eligibility:**
- US-based IHEs, nonprofits (associated with research/education), for-profits (US-owned >50%), state/local government, tribal nations
- PI must hold tenured/tenure-track position, full-time research/teaching appointment, or staff leadership in an Open-Source Program Office
- **Independent researchers cannot be sole applicants** -- must be affiliated with an eligible organization

**What POSE Funds:**
- Building managing organizations around existing open-source products
- Governance structures, community engagement, sustainability planning
- Does NOT fund development of open-source products themselves

**ORGANVM Fit:** EXCELLENT for Phase I. ORGANVM's 149-repo ecosystem with governance tooling (promotion state machines, registry management, dependency validation) is exactly what POSE targets. Frame as: "Building a sustainable open-source ecosystem for AI-orchestrated creative-institutional infrastructure." Would need institutional affiliation (university partner or a fiscal sponsor with eligible status).

## 2. NEA / NEH Grants

### CRITICAL WARNING: Federal Arts/Humanities Funding Under Threat

As of March 2026, the Trump administration has:
- Proposed elimination of both NEA and NEH in the FY2026 budget
- Cancelled 1,400+ open NEH grants (projects-in-progress)
- Issued grant termination notices at NEA
- Cancelled the Challenge America program
- The House proposed 35% budget cuts (from $207M to $135M each)

Congress has historically rebuffed elimination attempts (bipartisan support increased funding 2017-2020 during Trump's first term). The Senate passed level funding ($207M for NEH). **These programs still exist but are in flux.** Apply with awareness that awards may be delayed or rescinded.

## 6. University / Academic Pathways

### 6a. Can SGO Produce Recognized Academic Work?

**YES, with caveats.**

- Major publishers (Elsevier, Springer, IEEE, Wiley, Cambridge UP, IOP) accept "Independent Researcher" or "Independent Scholar" as valid affiliations
- Peer review evaluates work quality, not institutional prestige
- Double-anonymous review levels the playing field
- arXiv accepts preprints from independent researchers
- Over 40 papers have been published in discrete math by a single researcher without university affiliation
- SAGE Publishing explicitly guides independent scholars on publishing

**Key challenges:**
- Access to paywalled literature (mitigated by arXiv, open access, interlibrary loan)
- No institutional email (some journals still prefer .edu addresses)
- Grant applications often require institutional backing
- No IRB for human subjects research

### 6b. Research Affiliate Programs

Several universities offer Research Affiliate status to independent scholars:

- **University of Iowa** - Researcher Affiliate Program (CSSI)
- **East-West Center** - Affiliate Scholar Program
- **Various institutions** - Visiting/affiliate researcher positions

**Benefits of affiliation:**
- Submit grant proposals through the institution
- Access to facilities and resources
- Institutional email and library access
- FWA approval for human subjects research
- Credential for publication and conference participation

**How to obtain:**
- Contact department chairs or research center directors
- Propose a specific research collaboration
- Demonstrate existing body of work (ORGANVM's 117 repos, SGO papers)
- Offer something of value (novel methodology, technology platform, teaching)

### 6c. University Partnership Strategy for Grant Eligibility

The most practical path to NSF/NEH eligibility:

1. **Identify a faculty champion** at a research university whose work intersects with ORGANVM's domains (digital humanities, human-computer interaction, software engineering, media arts)
2. **Propose a co-PI arrangement** where the university submits the grant and you serve as senior personnel or co-PI
3. **Offer ORGANVM as a research platform** -- universities get access to a unique 149-repo ecosystem for studying open-source governance, AI-orchestrated development, or creative coding at scale
4. **Target departments:** Digital Humanities, Computer Science (SE/HCI), Media Arts, Information Science
5. **Start with smaller collaborations** (workshop, paper co-authorship) before attempting major grants

## 7. Fiscal Sponsorship Strategy

### The 501(c)(3) Problem

Many grants (NEA, some foundations) require 501(c)(3) status. Options:

| Option | Type | Status | Fees | Notes |
|--------|------|--------|------|-------|
| **Open Collective Foundation** | 501(c)(3) | **DISSOLVED** (Dec 2024) | N/A | No longer available |
| **Open Source Collective** | 501(c)(6) | Active | 10% of funds | NOT tax-deductible; NOT 501(c)(3) |
| **Software Freedom Conservancy** | 501(c)(3) | Active | Varies | Selective; FLOSS-focused; provides legal, financial, admin |
| **NumFOCUS** | 501(c)(3) | Active | 15% of funds | Science/data focused; tax-deductible donations |
| **Hack Club Bank** | 501(c)(3) | Active | 7% of funds | Youth/education focused |
| **Fractured Atlas** | 501(c)(3) | Active | 5-8% | Arts-focused fiscal sponsorship |

**Recommendation for ORGANVM:**
- **For arts grants (NEA etc.):** Fractured Atlas (arts-focused 501(c)(3))
- **For open-source grants (NSF POSE, NLnet):** Software Freedom Conservancy or form own 501(c)(3)
- **For science grants (CZI, NumFOCUS):** NumFOCUS
- **For immediate GitHub Sponsors:** Open Source Collective (501(c)(6), quick setup)

**IMPORTANT:** NEA explicitly prohibits fiscal sponsors. For NEA eligibility, ORGANVM would need its own 501(c)(3) with 3+ years of programming history.
