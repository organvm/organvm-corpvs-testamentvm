## Benefits Cliff Summary

> **The safety net is an asset, not a constraint to escape.**
> Understanding the cliff mechanics lets you pursue funding without losing essential benefits.

**Current situation:** Single adult, NYC, ~$12K adjunct base, receiving SNAP + Medicaid + Fair Fares.

| Program | Threshold | Grant Treatment | Risk Level |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **SNAP** | $20,352/yr (130% FPL) | **Lump-sum grants EXCLUDED entirely** (BBCE) | **SAFE** |
| **Medicaid** | $21,597/yr (138% FPL) | Taxable grants count toward MAGI | Moderate above ~$9.6K in grants |
| **Essential Plan** | $39,125/yr (250% FPL) | Same as Medicaid, but $0 premium bridge | Low — replaces Medicaid at no cost |
| **Fair Fares** | $22,692/yr (145% FPL) | Counts toward annual income | **Hard cliff** — loss costs ~$700-800/yr |
| **Section 8/NYCHA** | $56,700 (50% AMI) | One-time non-recurring excluded | **SAFE** |

**Safe grant-stacking ceiling:** ~$9K–$12K/year in one-time grants (on $12K adjunct base) preserves ALL benefits. With Schedule C deductions (art supplies, equipment, portfolio hosting), gross ceiling rises to ~$11K–$14K.

**If you exceed Medicaid threshold:** NYS Essential Plan covers you at **$0 premium** up to $39,125/year. The practical health coverage cliff is NOT at $21,597 — it's at $39,125.

**Key rule:** Call **NYLAG (212-613-5000)** before accepting any single grant >$5K. They provide free combined legal + financial counseling for exactly this situation.

## Recovery-Informed Staging

From FILE-060 clinical research: cognitive recovery after combined trauma follows a predictable curve.

### Months 1–3: Stabilization (NOW)

- **Focus:** Low-barrier submissions, infrastructure activation, benefits compliance
- **Actions:** GitHub Sponsors, Fractured Atlas, Artadia, NEH, Creative Fellowship
- **Capacity:** Reduced executive function — keep tasks concrete and time-bound
- **Rule:** No new named internal sprints (P0 hermetic seal)

### Months 3–6: Light Production

- **Focus:** Targeted applications, first consulting engagement, portfolio refinement
- **Actions:** Creative Capital, Fire Island, Spencer, Processing Foundation
- **Capacity:** Improving focus — can handle multi-day application processes
- **Rule:** One major submission per week maximum

### Months 6–12: Full Execution

- **Focus:** Career-building residencies, full consulting practice, strategic employment
- **Actions:** Tulsa, NEW INC, Pioneer Works, Queer|Art, conference submissions
- **Capacity:** Professional-quality sustained work
- **Rule:** Reassess benefits cliff position quarterly

## Decision Framework

When evaluating any new opportunity:

1. **Fit score ≥ 5/10?** If not, skip unless zero-effort (rolling applications).
2. **Benefits cliff impact?** Check against thresholds above. Lump-sum grants = SNAP safe. Recurring stipends = count as income.
3. **Recovery-stage appropriate?** Don't attempt 6-hour applications during stabilization phase.
4. **Identity position match?** Use the framing that matches the funder (educator / artist / architect / community).
5. **Deadline within capacity?** Only commit to deadlines you can actually meet.
