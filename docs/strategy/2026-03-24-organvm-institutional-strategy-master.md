## Executive Summary

ORGANVM already operates at institutional scale (117 repos, 8 organs, formal governance, internal university). What's missing is the **legal and financial infrastructure** that makes this fundable. The research converges on a single recommended path: **Fiscal Sponsorship + LLC** (scoring 33/35, highest of all 12 models analyzed).

This is a **modular institution** — some organs are charitable (research, art, education, community), others are commercial (SaaS tools, distribution services). The legal structure must mirror this modularity.

## The Structure: Two Entities, One System

```
FISCAL SPONSOR (e.g., Aspiration Tech)  ←  501(c)(3)
  │
  ├── ORGANVM Open Project (fiscally sponsored)
  │     ├── ORGAN-I  Theoria    — Academic research, SGO
  │     ├── ORGAN-II Poiesis    — Generative art, creative coding (open-source)
  │     ├── ORGAN-IV Taxis      — Governance tooling (open-source portion)
  │     ├── ORGAN-VI Koinonia   — Community, education, reading groups
  │     ├── META                — Registry, schemas, governance corpus
  │     │
  │     ├── Receives: grants, tax-deductible donations, sponsorships
  │     ├── Produces: open-source software, research papers, community programs
  │     └── IP: Apache 2.0 / MIT licensed, held by project
  │
  │
YOU (Individual)
  │
  └── ORGANVM LLC (for-profit, single-member)
        ├── ORGAN-III Ergon     — Commercial SaaS, developer tools, products
        ├── ORGAN-V  Logos      — Publishing (if revenue-generating)
        ├── ORGAN-VII Kerygma   — Distribution services, automation
        ├── Consulting, training, support contracts
        │
        ├── Revenue: product sales, subscriptions, consulting
        ├── Taxes: standard business taxation
        ├── IP: proprietary extensions built on open-source core
        └── Licenses open-source IP from sponsored project (free, via OSS license)
```

**Why this works:**
- Operational in 2-4 weeks, under $2,000 total cost
- No IRS approval needed (fiscal sponsor already has 501(c)(3))
- Immediately grant-eligible AND commercially active
- Solo-friendly: single-member LLC + sponsor handles nonprofit governance
- Clean IP: open-core model (open-source base + commercial layer)
- Scales to Mozilla model (Foundation + PBC) at Phase 3

## Legal Guardrails

### IRS Compliance (Private Benefit / Inurement)

- The nonprofit arm must serve **public benefit**, not primarily benefit the LLC
- Transactions between entities must be **arm's-length, fair market value**
- The LLC cannot siphon nonprofit funds; the nonprofit cannot exist primarily to serve the LLC
- Open-source licensing naturally handles this: anyone can use the open-source code, not just your LLC
- Keep **clean documentation** of the separation

### IP Strategy: Open-Core Model

```
OPEN SOURCE (Nonprofit / Sponsored Project)          PROPRIETARY (LLC)
├── organvm-engine (governance engine)                ├── Commercial SaaS dashboard
├── schema-definitions (JSON schemas)                 ├── Hosted orchestration service
├── organvm-mcp-server (MCP tools)                    ├── Enterprise support contracts
├── alchemia-ingestvm (ingestion pipeline)            ├── Premium integrations
├── Research papers, SGO academic output              ├── Training programs
├── Community tools, educational content              └── Proprietary extensions
└── All generative art tools (Organ II)
```

### What to Negotiate Upfront with Fiscal Sponsor

1. **IP ownership**: Project retains IP (prefer Model C) or clear IP transfer on exit (Model A)
2. **Exit terms**: Written agreement on asset transfer (cash, IP, trademarks, domains, donor lists)
3. **Commercial arm**: Explicit acknowledgment that a separate LLC operates commercially
4. **Notice period**: 60-90 days for spin-off
5. **Fund restrictions**: What can/cannot be spent from sponsored project funds

## What NOT to Do

1. **Do not form a 501(c)(3) now.** IRS is scrutinizing OSS nonprofits, processing takes 6-10 months, and you need 3 unrelated board members. Fiscal sponsorship gets you there in weeks.
2. **Do not use an L3C.** Too obscure, limited state availability, no real tax benefits, weak brand recognition.
3. **Do not form a cooperative.** Requires multiple worker-owners. Contradicts solo practitioner model.
4. **Do not use the Linux Foundation model (501(c)(6)).** Requires membership base. Donations not tax-deductible.
5. **Do not apply to Fractured Atlas.** Non-commercial requirement disqualifies ORGANVM's full scope.
6. **Do not apply to NEA.** Requires 501(c)(3) with 3-5 year programming history AND prohibits fiscal sponsors.
7. **Do not wait.** NLnet deadline is April 1. Creative Capital is April 2. Shuttleworth is May 1.
