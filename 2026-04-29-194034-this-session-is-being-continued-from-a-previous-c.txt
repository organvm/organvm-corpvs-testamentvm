  вҳ… Insight в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
  - Compaction is a context-window operation, not a storage operation. The JSONL
   on disk is append-only вҖ” /compact writes a summary record into it but doesn't
   truncate prior turns. What changes is which records get replayed into 
  Claude's context on the next turn: post-compact, only the summary + new turns
  are loaded; the older verbatim turns are skipped over (still on disk, just not
   in working memory).
  - Resume reads the live tail, not the archive. claude --resume re-enters at
  the post-compact state because that's the last context configuration written.
  The pre-compact turns are forensically recoverable (you can jq through the
  JSONL), but Claude on resume does not see them вҖ” only the summary plus
  anything after it.
  - Cross-session persistence is a different mechanism entirely. CLAUDE.md
  (project + global), MEMORY.md, and your output style are re-injected from disk
   every session start, regardless of compaction. That's why the guidance to
  save artifact-paths-and-state to project_artifact_*.md matters вҖ” those survive
   compactions and session boundaries because they live outside the JSONL.
